About Security Council


In a fast paced world where the lines between right and wrong get blurred, it is the Security Council's job to determine the existence of a threat to peace or an act of aggression. A process, which requires the delegates to look at a situation from all perspectives, so that there is a better world tomorrow.

Note from the Executive Board


Dear All,
I, Angad Singh Madan, will be coming to BITS Hyderabad MUN for the very first time.I would continue the jargon about “expectancy of high level of debate and participation”, but I would rather tell you what an MUN is all about and I don’t intend to talk about procedure or ways to research. Must I say, the International politics has more to it that the UN itself and much negotiation and lobbying happens outside the walls of the UN.

During my experience of over 5 years of enjoying doing MUNs I have dwelled not only in the deeper aspects of research but found myself enjoying the international politics. I look forward to having “good" delegates in my committee, but recently have understood that no delegate is established "good" by default and the past MUN awards really have no say in the MUN you are attending currently.

An MUN is all about being the delegate you would want to see the Best Delegate award being given to. Having said that, the agenda suits the needs of politics hungry legal debaters who love to formulate defense related policy and I am hopeful that even the people who are doing an MUN for the first time would still love it. The presence of Yash Karunakaran and Arvind Srinivasan on the judging and guiding panel not only has added substance to the council but I know that they would be there to guide you at all stages through the committee.

Besides, when I do MUNs as a delegate (which I still love doing), my only aim is to interact with new people and at times sweep their feet off the ground with an argument. I am to assure you that nothing changes when I chair, except I would want to watch the same enthusiasm from you all rather than I making arguments.

I hope we would be interacting post the council and chilling all along.
Also, I love beer. Take the hint.

Angad Singh Madan
Chairperson
UNSC, BITSMUN'15
